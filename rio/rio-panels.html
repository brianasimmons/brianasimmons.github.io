Transatlantic Trade and the Marvelous City: Material Culture in Nineteenth-Century Rio de Janeiro  maps the nineteenth-century, transatlantic trade of material culture to and from Brazil. It uses shipping records, inventories, and city maps to chart the flow of goods, and show how international exchange shaped the development of Rio de Janeiroâ€™s urban environment. The data constitutes a compilation of primary sources which are placed in geographic and chronological context.

This application shows how the transatlantic exchange of goods transformed Rio de Janeiro from a provincial port into a modern metropolis, and demonstrates that certain areas of the city developed according to the types of goods that were traded there.

The original unreferenced map, Nova planta da cidade do Rio de Janeiro, A'venda em casa dos editores, E. & H. Laemmert, 1867 is available from the Library of Congress at https://www.loc.gov/item/2003682769/.   Get map data and code from my GitHub page.
